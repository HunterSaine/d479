FAQ

What is Taniti?

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

How do I get there?

There are several ways to get to Taniti. You can fly into Taniti International Airport, which is located on the island. You can also travel here by ship. Once you arrive on the island, you can rent a car or take a taxi to your destination.

What should I bring?

You should pack clothes for a variety of outdoor activities suuch as swimming, hiking, and diving. Taniti's power outlets are 120 volts(The same as the United States), so make sure you bring the right adapters.

What is the weather like?

The weather in Taniti is warm and tropical year-round. The average temperature is around 80 degrees Fahrenheit, with little variation throughout the year. The island does experience a rainy season from November to April, but the rain is usually short and heavy, followed by clear skies.

What are the best things to do in Taniti?

There are many things to do in Taniti, from relaxing on the beach to exploring the island's natural beauty. Some popular activities include snorkeling, scuba diving, hiking, and visiting the local volcano. You can also take a boat tour around the island or relax in a beachside hammock with a tropical drink in hand.

Where can I buy groceries?

Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.

What's the food like?

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants: five serve mostly local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.
