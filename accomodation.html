Transportation

Taniti is primarily accessible by air, with a small cruise ship making weekly one-night stops in Yellow Leaf Bay [1]. The island is served by a well-equipped airport that caters to small jets and propeller planes, with an expansion underway to accommodate larger jets in the coming years [1].

Getting around Taniti is a breeze. Public buses operate within Taniti City from 5 am to 11 pm daily [1]. Private buses service the outlying areas. Taxis are readily available in Taniti City, and car rentals can be arranged through a local agency near the airport [1]. Biking is a popular option, with helmets mandatory by law. Helmets and bikes can be rented from several vendors scattered throughout the island [1]. Taniti City itself is known for being flat and easily walkable [1]. Many tourist accommodations are concentrated around Merriton Landing, making it a pedestrian-friendly paradise [1].
